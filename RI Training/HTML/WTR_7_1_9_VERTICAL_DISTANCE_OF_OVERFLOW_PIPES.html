WTR 7.1.9 - Vertical distance of overflow pipes and inlet pipe in the Water Storage Tank

3.1.9 Every inlet pipe to a water storage tank, whether fitted with a float-operated valve or not, shall be fitted in such a position that it discharges at a level higher than the overflowing level of the overflow pipe or, if there is more than one overflow pipe, the highest overflow pipe, by not less than the diameter of that overflow pipe, unless there is an effective means of preventing the siphonage of water through the inlet.
